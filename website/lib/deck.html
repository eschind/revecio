ReveWhy it's broken

The status quo

Analyst-heavy, premium-priced, structurally underperforming.

  • High-cost, human-intensive. Every IC package, monitoring report, scenario analysis, and board deck is produced manually. Technology leverage is near zero.
  • Premium pricing. Sub-$250M clients pay 30–40 bps blended; fully loaded fees reach 75–175 bps — consuming budget that would otherwise fund mission.
  • Dispersion and underperformance. 8% spread between top- and bottom-quartile OCIO returns over three years through 2025 — a 25% compounded gap. Bottom quartile returned just 3% in 2024.
  • Opacity and conflicts. Hidden fees, cherry-picked track records, and compensation routed through affiliated products are pervasive.
  • Smaller institutions excluded. ~60% of the $1.25T sub-$250M segment is unaddressed by any third-party investment management service.

ReveWhy now

OCIO is the next frontier for agentic AI

90% of OCIO work is information labor, not judgment labor.

Gathering market and manager data, building and stress-testing portfolios, drafting IPS documents, monitoring positions, generating board materials, answering trustee questions — all dense information processing, today done by hand.

Frontier agentic systems with the right data foundation can do nearly all of it, with full audit trails. The remaining 10% — investment philosophy, major allocations, client trust, high-stakes governance — stays with humans.

Agentic OCIO is harder than agentic legal or accounting — more degrees of freedom, fewer clean right answers. That difficulty creates a greater moat than those of existing AI-enabled service businesses.
